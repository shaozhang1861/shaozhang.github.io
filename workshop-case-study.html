Case Study

Designing for Scale

How we turned live-stream commerce into a data-driven engine

What I've Done

When I joined the Meituan Live team, most hosts manually arranged their product lists before going live. It was inefficient and inconsistent — a host's personal judgment decided which items appeared first, regardless of user context.

I led the initiative to make the ranking intelligent. We started with data exploration: which factors truly predicted a user's purchase intent? Distance within 3 km, coupon availability, chain-store reputation, and users' saved favorites turned out to be strong signals.

Through four A/B test cycles, we replaced intuition with algorithms. The result: +9,000 daily orders, a statistically significant uplift that exceeded most hosts' manual performance.

Reflections

The hardest part wasn't technical — it was cultural. Convincing non-technical stakeholders that a model could outperform human judgment required empathy and transparency.

I learned that "explainable automation" builds trust faster than any metric chart.

My Takeaways

  • Lead with a hypothesis, not a feature. People buy into why before what.
  • Show incremental wins. Each iteration should visibly reduce friction for real users.
  • Translate data into narrative. Explain algorithms in human terms — it earns adoption.
